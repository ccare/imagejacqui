About Jacqui


Jacqui is very keen on digital photography and the use of photographs in a variety of contexts including commercial, educational, and spiritual. She is also a Methodist Minister currently living and working in Norfolk.

In 2006 Jacqui spent nearly a year living on the island of Iona in Argyll, Scotland, working for the Iona Community. If you would like a copy of her booklet ‘Living Iona’ (which chronicles her experiences through a series of letters complemented by poems and photographs) please contact her by emailing jacqui.a.horton@googlemail.com

Whilst on Iona, Jacqui became interested in birds – especially if they were near enough to photograph! She is gradually building up a collection of bird photos that are suitable to use on greeting cards.

Whilst living in Hampshire from 2007 – 2010, Jacqui pursued her interest in the life of Jane Austen. She developed a talk and presentation entitled ‘Jane Austen in Hampshire’ and a version of this will hopefully soon be available in booklet format. Again, if you are interested, please email.

Jacqui currently uses two cameras: a Nikon ‘bridge’ camera – Coolpix P100 (26x zoom) and a Sony digital SLR – Alpha 200 (with 3.5 – 70 mm and 4.5 – 300 mm lenses). She has three ‘golden’ rules for taking photographs:

  1. Always have the camera
  2. Seize the moment (the blue sky may disappear!)
  3. Take several photos then choose the best
